NGO Worker

Why?

Working in an NGO is a fulfilling career that involves addressing social issues, advocating for change, and improving lives.

Opportunities

NGO workers can specialize in areas like education, healthcare, or environmental conservation. Opportunities also exist in fundraising and program management.

Strengths/Preparation

Empathy, communication skills, and a passion for social work are essential. A degree in social work or related fields is beneficial.

Colleges

Top institutions include TISS, JNU, and University of Sussex.

Exams

No specific exams, but certifications in social work and project management are valuable.

Additional Information

NGO workers need to stay updated with social policies and continuously enhance their skills through training and workshops.
