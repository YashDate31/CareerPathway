Anthropologist

Why Become an Anthropologist?

Anthropologists study human societies, cultures, and their development over time. It is a fascinating career for those interested in understanding human behavior and history.

Opportunities

Anthropologists can work in academia, museums, research institutions, and NGOs. They can also specialize in areas like archaeology, cultural anthropology, and forensic anthropology.

Strengths/Preparation

Strong analytical skills, curiosity, and a background in social sciences are essential. Fieldwork and research experience are highly valuable in this field.

Top Institutes

  • University of Delhi
  • Jawaharlal Nehru University (JNU), New Delhi
  • Banaras Hindu University (BHU), Varanasi
  • University of Calcutta

Entrance Exams

  • CUET (Common University Entrance Test)
  • State-level entrance exams for social sciences

Additional Information

Anthropology is a multidisciplinary field that often involves collaboration with historians, sociologists, and scientists. Staying updated with global research trends is crucial for career growth.
