What Does a Doctor Do?

Doctors are healthcare professionals dedicated to diagnosing, treating, and preventing illnesses and injuries. They care for patients, promote health, and improve lives through medical knowledge and compassionate service.

Career Path to Becoming a Doctor

  1. Complete secondary education with a focus on science subjects like Biology, Chemistry, and Physics.
  2. Enroll in a recognized medical college to earn an MBBS or equivalent degree.
  3. Complete internship and residency programs for hands-on clinical training.
  4. Obtain the required medical license and certifications.
  5. Optionally, specialize in fields such as surgery, pediatrics, cardiology, or general practice.

Skills and Qualities

  • Strong knowledge of medical science and human anatomy
  • Excellent communication and interpersonal skills
  • Empathy and compassion for patients
  • Critical thinking and problem-solving abilities
  • Patience and ability to work under pressure

Why Choose to Become a Doctor?

Being a doctor is a rewarding career with the opportunity to make a real difference in people's lives. It offers a blend of science, service, and continuous learning. If you are passionate about health and helping others, this path is ideal.
