Linguist

Why?

Linguistics is a fascinating career that involves studying languages, their structure, and their impact on communication and culture.

Opportunities

Linguists can work in academia, translation, or as language consultants. Opportunities also exist in computational linguistics and language preservation.

Strengths/Preparation

Strong analytical skills, a passion for languages, and research abilities are essential. A degree in linguistics or related fields is beneficial.

Colleges

Top institutions include JNU, EFLU, and MIT.

Exams

Entrance exams like UGC NET (for teaching and research) and university-specific tests are common for linguistics programs.

Additional Information

Linguists need to stay updated with advancements in language technology and continuously enhance their skills through research and certifications.
