Marine Biologist

Why?

Marine biology is a fascinating career that involves studying marine organisms and their ecosystems to understand and protect ocean life.

Opportunities

Marine biologists can work in research institutions, conservation organizations, or as educators. Opportunities also exist in aquaculture and marine policy-making.

Strengths/Preparation

Strong analytical skills, a passion for marine life, and fieldwork experience are essential. A degree in marine biology or related fields is beneficial.

Colleges

Top institutions include CIFE, Scripps Institution of Oceanography, and University of Miami.

Exams

Entrance exams like GATE (for marine biology) and university-specific tests are common for marine biology programs.

Additional Information

Marine biologists need to stay updated with advancements in marine science and continuously enhance their skills through research and certifications.
