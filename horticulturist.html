Horticulturist

Why?

Horticulture is a rewarding career that involves cultivating plants, fruits, vegetables, and flowers to enhance food production and beautify landscapes.

Opportunities

Horticulturists can work in agricultural research, landscaping, or as consultants. Opportunities also exist in floriculture and organic farming.

Strengths/Preparation

Knowledge of plant science, soil management, and pest control is essential. A degree in horticulture or related fields is beneficial.

Colleges

Top institutions include TNAU, IARI, and Cornell University.

Exams

Entrance exams like ICAR AIEEA and university-specific tests are common for horticulture programs.

Additional Information

Horticulturists need to stay updated with advancements in agricultural technology and continuously enhance their skills through training and certifications.
