Human Rights Activist

Why?

Human rights activism is a noble career that involves advocating for justice, equality, and the protection of fundamental rights for all individuals.

Opportunities

Human rights activists can work with NGOs, international organizations like the UN, or as independent advocates. Opportunities also exist in policy-making and legal advocacy.

Strengths/Preparation

Strong communication, empathy, and knowledge of human rights laws are essential. A degree in law, social work, or related fields is beneficial.

Colleges

Top institutions include JNU, TISS, and Harvard Kennedy School.

Exams

Entrance exams like CLAT (for law) and university-specific tests are common for human rights programs.

Additional Information

Human rights activists need to stay updated with global issues and continuously enhance their skills through workshops and advocacy training.
